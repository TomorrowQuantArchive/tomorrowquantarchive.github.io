Welcome to Tomorrow Quant Archive — offering clarity in complexity.

Tomorrow Quant Archive is an unofficial guide to quantitative finance, built for aspiring quants, university students, and practitioners who want structured, practical, and intellectually honest material on trading, research, and market structure.

The archive brings together the work behind Ctrl+Alt+DefeatTheMarket, originally created for participants in IMC Prosperity 4, and the earlier KnowledgeBase developed for readers in the University of Copenhagen Quant, Mathematics, and Actuarial community. It is designed to make complex material easier to navigate without diluting the depth that serious learners require.

About this archive

This site is a curated home for quantitative finance resources, combining explanatory notes, practical code, visual intuition, and research-oriented structure. The aim is to help readers move from scattered information to a clearer understanding of how quantitative ideas are developed, tested, and applied.

Who it is for

  • Students preparing for quant internships, competitions, and research roles
  • Aspiring quants building practical foundations in trading and data analysis
  • Industry professionals looking for concise strategy notes and technical refreshers
  • Self-directed learners who prefer substance over hype

What to expect

The archive will expand into dedicated sections for market making, statistical arbitrage, order book analysis, backtesting, options, research templates, and trading-challenge writeups. The material will avoid proprietary work while aiming to get as close as responsibly possible to useful, practical insight.
