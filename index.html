What is this guide?

A structured archive for navigating quantitative finance.

Tomorrow Quant Archive is an unofficial guide to quantitative finance, built for aspiring quants, university students, and practitioners who want structured, practical, and intellectually honest material on trading, research, and market structure.

The archive is an extensive range of pages, curated resources, visualisations, and guided next-step processes. It is intended to help readers prepare for trading competitions, understand interview expectations, discover useful communities, and build the baseline technical understanding required to move seriously into quantitative finance.

You will find guidance on competitions, reading paths, interviews, videos, channels, Discord servers, forums, research workflows, and practical code. The purpose is not to sell certainty, but to reduce unnecessary confusion and help serious learners build momentum faster.

Structured learning

Clear paths through topics that are often scattered across books, blogs, forums, lectures, and code repositories.

Practical orientation

Material focused on research habits, strategy thinking, implementation, backtesting, and competition preparation.

Responsible depth

Useful public material without sharing proprietary work or pretending that complex fields can be reduced to shortcuts.

Who is it for?

For learners who want to become useful quickly — without skipping the fundamentals.

The archive serves as a unified home for the previous work on Ctrl+Alt+DefeatTheMarket and the KnowledgeBase. Its main purpose is to build a practical highway from early curiosity to baseline competence in quantitative finance.

It has been our experience that even passionate graduates often struggle to identify the baseline assumptions, workflows, and procedures expected in algorithmic trading, quantitative research, and market-facing technical roles. This archive aims to make those expectations more visible.

The first goal is to help aspiring quants get up to speed quickly. The second is to present useful, relevant, and technically grounded quant work that readers can learn from, adapt, and build upon.

Getting started

A practical first route through the archive.

If you are new to quantitative finance, the best starting point is not to memorise isolated formulas. Start by building a working map: what the field contains, what roles require, what skills transfer, and how research becomes a decision-making process.

1

Understand the landscape

Learn the difference between quantitative research, trading, development, risk, execution, market making, and data roles.

2

Build the foundations

Strengthen probability, statistics, programming, data handling, market mechanics, and basic financial instruments.

3

Work through projects

Use competitions, simulations, backtests, notebooks, and small strategy prototypes to turn theory into usable judgment.

4

Prepare for selection

Study interview patterns, mental maths, probability questions, coding exercises, research discussions, and trading intuition.

A strong reader should eventually be able to explain a strategy, test it, identify its weaknesses, understand its market assumptions, and communicate the trade-off between risk, signal quality, implementation, and execution.

Challenges

Competitions as a practical bridge between theory and trading.

Trading and quantitative research challenges are useful because they force ideas to meet constraints. You have limited information, noisy signals, changing market conditions, position limits, execution costs, and competitors adapting around you.

The archive will use competition-style problems as a practical training ground for market making, statistical arbitrage, options thinking, risk management, and systematic debugging. The goal is not only to improve leaderboard performance, but to understand what a strategy is actually doing and why it fails.

Before the challenge

Build foundations, review previous competitions, prepare reusable code structures, and practise reading market data.

During the challenge

Analyse data quickly, form hypotheses, test strategies, control risk, and avoid overfitting to misleading signals.

After the challenge

Write post-mortems, compare approaches, clean up code, extract lessons, and convert the work into portfolio evidence.

The layers of quant

A layered way to understand the field.

Quantitative finance is easier to approach when it is separated into layers. The archive will gradually organise material around these layers so readers can identify what they already know, what they are missing, and what to study next.

Foundations

Probability, statistics, linear algebra, optimisation, stochastic processes, programming, and data handling.

Markets

Instruments, exchanges, order books, liquidity, spreads, volatility, execution, and market microstructure.

Research

Hypothesis generation, exploratory analysis, signal design, validation, feature engineering, and avoiding false discovery.

Strategies

Market making, mean reversion, trend following, statistical arbitrage, options, hedging, and portfolio construction.

Implementation

Backtesting, simulation, production constraints, code structure, logging, monitoring, and reproducible workflows.

Risk and judgment

Position limits, drawdowns, regime changes, transaction costs, model risk, behavioural errors, and decision quality.

Career translation

Interviews, projects, competitions, CV evidence, research writeups, networking, and communicating technical ability.
